Our Story

Our journey began with a simple connection that quickly turned into a love filled with laughter and adventure. Now, as we start this new chapter, we can't wait to celebrate with the ones we love most.

  • Prince POV

    I met my wife to be in 2017 when i was assigned to pastor a student fellowship church in Awka, Anambra State. As a passionate man of God, I was focused on raising young and vibrant individuals for Jesus. One faithful evening, while I was counselling several people facing various challenges, praying for them, laying hands on them, and encouraging them, a lovely innocent and beautiful lady approached me. At first, i didnt take much notice of her. I counseled her, and as weeks and months passed, she was elected to a leadership position in the fellowship
    During that time, i had caught the vision for the inner circle, which eventually became House of Redemption today. I brought her into that inner circle along with others and began to mentor them. I never imagined that she would one day become my wife; I was solely focused on bringing out the best in her. And here we are today.
    I love you, my chocolate brown fine wine!

  • Confidence POV

    We met in 2017 when I was still looking for admission. I was definitely not looking for 'man'. It was admission that carried me to Awka, little did I know that God was putting things in place for me beyond admission. I was doing the Unizik prescience program in Mbaukwu(MBK), Anambra State and happened to choose RCF MBK as my fellowship. He was sent there as an assistant coordinator to pastor us. He particularly interested me because he can teach God's word. Like the man has the gift of teaching. It reminded me of my Dad.
    But being the reserved person I was, I kept a healthy distance from him until the day it was impressed in my spirit to share something with him. He gave me his counsel, prayed with me and that was it until I was made the Bible Study coordinator directly reporting to him. We got closer after I organized a Sunday school department program. He took a special interest in mentoring me. I remember him saying to me, "do you know you are very administrative?" Well, I knew I was good at organizing things and programs but that was the first time anyone was putting it that way. I started seeing myself in a new light from that day. As for him? He made it his business to nurture all my strengths... He kept giving me one responsibility to another. And I kept growing in the Lord. Looking back now, I really didn't stand a chance. God was working it out every step of the way.
    I love you so much, my pastor!
